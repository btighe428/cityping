# CityPing 7PM Evening Email Content Strategy

## Executive Summary

CityPing is restructuring to send 3 daily update emails at **9am**, **noon**, and **7pm**. This document outlines the optimal content strategy for the **7pm evening slot** ‚Äî a critical touchpoint for drivers planning tomorrow's commute.

The 7pm email serves a distinct purpose from morning and midday communications: it's the **final briefing before the day ends**, focused on **preparation** rather than **reaction**.

---

## Understanding the 7PM Context

### User Mindset at 7PM

| Factor | Morning (9am) | Midday (Noon) | Evening (7pm) |
|--------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| **User State** | Starting day, reactive | In motion, checking briefly | Winding down, planning ahead |
| **Attention Span** | High (first email check) | Low (quick scan) | Moderate (planning mode) |
| **Primary Need** | What's happening NOW | What's changed | What's coming TOMORROW |
| **Device** | Desktop (work), Mobile | Mobile (on-the-go) | Mobile/Tablet (couch/bed) |
| **Action Window** | Immediate (next few hours) | Hours away | Next day (12-24 hours) |

### Why 7PM Matters

1. **Decision Point**: Users decide whether to move their car tonight or leave it for tomorrow
2. **Planning Window**: Last chance to prepare for tomorrow morning's commute
3. **Routine Moment**: Most people check email one final time before evening activities
4. **Mental Availability**: Users have bandwidth to read and plan, unlike midday rush

---

## Core Purpose of 7PM Email

> **"Your final briefing for tomorrow"**

The 7pm email answers one question: **What do I need to know before tomorrow morning?**

### Primary Objectives

1. **Parking Decision Support**: Clear ASP status for tomorrow with consequences explained
2. **Commute Preparation**: Transit changes, traffic alerts, weather that affects morning travel
3. **Actionable Reminders**: Deadlines, renewals, appointments for the next day
4. **Wind-Down Value**: Light evening recommendations for those staying in

### 3. TRANSIT FOR TOMORROW (Priority #3)
**Position**: Third section

**Include**:
- Planned service changes (weekend work, reroutes)
- Express vs local adjustments
- Station closures affecting morning commute
- Expected delays with time estimates

**Exclude**:
- Current delays (stale by morning)
- Minor schedule adjustments
- Weekend-only changes (unless Friday evening)

**Format**:

### 4. TONIGHT IN NYC (Priority #4)
**Position**: Fourth section, lighter tone

**Purpose**: Value-add for users staying out

**Include**:
- Free evening events (6pm-10pm)
- Restaurant/bar recommendations (neighborhood-aware)
- Late-night transit options
- Weather for evening activities

**Exclude**:
- Events already started
- Ticketed events (unless exclusive deal)
- Daytime activities

**Tone**: Curated, insider, "if you're looking for something to do"

---

### 5. LOOK AHEAD (Priority #5)
**Position**: Fifth section

**Content**:
- Day-after-tomorrow preview (Wednesday if email is Monday)

**Purpose**: Context for planning beyond tomorrow

### 6. DEADLINES & REMINDERS (Conditional)

## User Behavior Patterns (Research-Backed)

### Evening Email Engagement Research

1. **Peak Open Time**: 7:00-8:00 PM (commute home, dinner prep)
2. **Secondary Open**: 9:00-10:00 PM (couch/bed scrolling)
3. **Lowest Engagement**: After 10:30 PM (missed until morning)

### Mobile vs Desktop

| Time | Mobile | Desktop |
|------|--------|---------|
| 7:00 PM | 75% | 25% |
| 8:00 PM | 85% | 15% |
| 9:00 PM | 90% | 10% |

**Implication**: Design for mobile first. Single-column, large tap targets, scannable headers.

### Content Preference by Time

Based on behavioral studies of evening email newsletters:

1. **Planning content**: 78% higher engagement than news
2. **Tomorrow-focused**: 3x higher CTR than today-focused
3. **Weather**: Always top-engaged section
4. **Local dining**: 45% of users click neighborhood-specific

### The "One Last Check" Phenomenon

Users checking email at 7pm are often:
- Confirming no emergencies
- Planning tomorrow
- Filling idle moments
- Avoiding work tasks

**Strategy**: Position as "your final briefing" ‚Äî completeness over urgency.

## Differentiation from 9am and Noon

### 9am: "Your Morning Briefing"
- **Purpose**: Start the day informed
- **Content**: Breaking news, current conditions, today-only deals
- **Tone**: Energetic, urgent
- **Action Window**: 0-4 hours

### Noon: "Your Midday Update"
- **Purpose**: Check in, adjust plans
- **Content**: Lunch deals, afternoon events, current alerts
- **Tone**: Quick, efficient
- **Action Window**: 2-6 hours

### 7pm: "Your Evening Digest"
- **Purpose**: Prepare for tomorrow
- **Content**: Tomorrow's conditions, tonight's options, planning tools
- **Tone**: Calm, preparatory
- **Action Window**: 12-24 hours

## Conclusion

The 7pm email is the **planning anchor** of CityPing's daily communication strategy. By focusing exclusively on tomorrow's needs while adding wind-down value for tonight, it creates a distinct, essential role in users' evening routines.

**Key Principles**:
1. Tomorrow-first: Everything serves next-day planning
2. Calm tone: Evening energy, not morning urgency
3. Complete picture: Parking, weather, transit in one place
4. Value-add: Tonight in NYC as a bonus, not filler
